My Story

Coming from rural Ireland, I've worked diligently and simulataneously at many jobs to support myself throughout my education, from waitressing and bartending to business development, art class assistant, Maths tuition and cutting turf (the Irish will know!). My commitment to each role has been duly recognised by my employeers and although many have looked for me to return over the years, it was the offer to step into full time software development with Breakthrough Maths that peaked my interest. Although I had learnt some coding in my Engineering degrees, I had no experience in web development, yet the challenge excited me. I had never even heard of ClojureScript or React a year ago, or even used IDEs such as VS Code, but now I have the confidence and assurance in my abilities to produce new suites of components, design and work with extremely nested data structures, build and maintain code. ClojureScript is a functional, lisp based language that may not be widely used, however it is my belief that a good software engineer should be able to adapt to or learn any language, a skill which over the past year I've proven myself willing to do, and at considerable pace. I am driven, hardworking, excited in the face of a challenge and relentless in the pursuit of personal improvement.

Education

I graduated from Trinity College Dublin in 2023 with a first class honours in Engineering with Management, MAI.

My masters thesis studied the microstructure and mechanical properties of 3D printed Ti64 for use in orthopaedic implants. I undertook another masters thesis equivalent during my study abroad at INSA Lyon, France which focused on the biomechanics and wear of hip implants.

Although these works were centered around materials, I particularly enjoyed other modules I took such as computational engineering, thermodynamics and thermofluids, matlab based projects, probability and statistics, electronics and user centered design for innovation.

SUAGR Network

My final year at Trinity also seen me selected to join the SUGAR network, a global design thinking network bringing together the world’s future makers, universities and companies for a new learning experience. Together with the University of São Paulo, our team was tasked with finding an equitable way of shifting daily road user way mobility patterns to more sustainable solutions, in a way that facilitates widespread deployment while minimising dedicated infrastructure. Breakthrough Maths

Breakthrough Maths began for me as one of many jobs to help pay for my Masters degree, yet grew into something so much more. Inspired by the tight knit, driven team created by our CEO TJ, the company ethos and their desire to create real change in the world of Maths education, I quickly rose through the ranks; from hand cranking slide decks to building software with the power to generate more engaging content and much, much more. Now our platform works to provide kids learning maths with a pedagogically curated way of approaching Maths, with engagment throughout each step at it's core. Despite having already taught almost 8000 students on a weekly basis in Ireland, the real end goal of this company is still being realised, with many big things still in the pipeline.

Innovation. Collaboration. Start-up culture. Adaptability.

Working in a small team of 3 software engineers, I have benefitted emmensely from the wealth of experience from our CTO and the opportunity to work on a wide range of problems. I've also found myself involved in onboarding new team members, shaping company processes, influencing and making key decisions around the product's direction, joining meetings with the CEO and external stakeholders, including beta testers. Through these experiences I've gained an holistic understanding of how various functions interconnect and contribute to the overall success of a company. All in all, this hands-on journey has not only honed my technical skills but also cultivated my ability to adapt and innovate in dynamic environments, ensuring that I can contribute effectively across different facets of an organization.
